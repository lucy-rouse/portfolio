All About Junk Journaling

An informational website by Lucy Rouse

What is Junk Journaling?

Junk Journaling is a type of journaling where you collect the small (usually paper) items from your life that would have otherwise been thrown away, and you glue them into your journal. Examples of the random items could include reciepts, concert tickets, movie tickets, straw wrappers, stickers, tags, and so on. It tells the story of your life without you having to write everything down.

A Brief History

Junk Journaling origionated on a social media platform called TikTok in 2021. It stemmed from the orgional form of journaling: writing your thoughts down on a piece of paper. That started a trend of "bullet journaling," a form of journaling which includes more artistry and creativity. Eventually, junk journaling was born.

Ideas of What to Journal

1. A gallery of your favorite stickers!

A lot of times, deciding where to put a sticker is stressful. I'm always worried about disliking where I end up putting it, and regretting my choice. This way, you can dedicate a specific page in your journal for your favorite stickers.

2. A showcase for some of your favorite song lyrics!

You can print out screenshots of the words to your favorite songs and decorate the page with those. You could even include song lyric analysis or a little bit about what the song means to you.

3. A page dedicated to movie/concert tickets!

If you're someone who goes to the movies or concerts a lot, having a page in your journal dedicated to those tickets could be a cool way to reflect on the awesome art you've seen.
